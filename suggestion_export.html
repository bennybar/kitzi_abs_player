Kitzi Direction From Reference

Yes, there are a few things here worth taking. The value is in the home-to-player system, not in copying the whole visual style.

The screenshot gets three things right: it makes listening progress feel rewarding, it keeps the player present without taking over, and it uses shelves/cards to create a calm audiobook-first home. For Kitzi, I would adopt those structural ideas, keep the dark immersive tone, and avoid copying the cramped text sizing and generic utility clutter.

What To Take

Adopt

Make home progress-driven

Bring listening time, streak, and the current book closer to the top. This gives Kitzi a stronger reason to reopen daily and immediately makes playback history feel alive.

Adopt

Dock the mini player above navigation

The floating bar is the strongest pattern in the shot. It reads as persistent, tactile, and clearly linked to the current book without eating vertical space.

Adopt

Use shelves instead of dense lists

Small horizontal rails for Continue Listening and Recently Added fit audiobook browsing better than a flat feed and create better cover visibility.

Adopt

Keep the dark cinema-like canvas

A deep black-to-warm-charcoal background works well for book art and makes the player feel premium. This matches the more immersive direction already present in Kitzi.

What Not To Copy

Skip

Text that is too small and too dim

The screenshot looks polished, but some metadata is close to decorative. Kitzi should keep the hierarchy, but raise contrast and size so the app stays readable during actual use.

Skip

Too many generic icon circles

The top-right actions are visually neat but semantically vague. Kitzi should keep one clear utility action or move these controls into a more explicit overflow surface.

Skip

Replica-level imitation of another app

The goal should be: same clarity and pacing, different identity. Keep Kitzi’s Google Sans core, warmer materials, and the stronger playback affordances already in the app.

This mockup keeps the reference composition but translates it into a likely final Kitzi home surface: stronger stat cards, more legible metadata, and a more intentional mini player dock.

Best idea in the reference: the mini player and nav behave like one bottom system. That is the piece most worth lifting into Kitzi.
